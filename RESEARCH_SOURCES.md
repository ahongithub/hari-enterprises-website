## Industry & category knowledge (general, vendor-neutral)
General refractory-materials domain knowledge was used to write technically
responsible product overviews, industry/application pages and knowledge-centre
articles (types of refractories, castable vs brick selection, ceramic fibre
properties, anchor selection, causes of refractory failure, RFQ guidance).
No specific numeric specifications were invented; grades/dimensions are presented
as "available on enquiry."

## SEO keyword research
Buyer-intent keyword themes were mapped from the brief and category logic
(e.g. "refractory material supplier Ahmedabad/Gujarat", "fire brick supplier",
"castable cement supplier", "ceramic blanket supplier", "refractory anchor
supplier"). See `SEO_STRATEGY.md` for the full keyword map.

## Benchmarking approach
Information architecture and the buyer journey were designed against common
patterns of Indian and international refractory/industrial B2B suppliers
(homepage → product taxonomy → product detail → RFQ; industry/application pages;
leadership; local trust signals). No competitor's design, text or assets were copied.

## Honesty guardrails applied
Not claimed anywhere on the site (no supporting evidence): manufacturing plants,
production capacity, certifications, laboratory/testing, export presence, branch
network, employee counts, revenue, authorized distributorship or exclusive
partnerships, client testimonials, or specific technical specifications.
